## Background
### XOR-based Merkle Aggregation
XOR-based Merkle aggregation is a cryptographic construct that allows efficient verification of data integrity across distributed systems. By using XOR operations, this method effectively combines multiple Merkle trees, ensuring that any discrepancies in data can be quickly identified through the root hash.

### Content-Addressable Anti-Entropy Mechanism
Content-addressable anti-entropy mechanisms facilitate the synchronization of distributed data by allowing nodes to identify and share content based on its unique hash. This method improves data consistency across nodes while minimizing bandwidth usage during synchronization processes.

3. **Robustness**: XOR-based aggregation provides strong guarantees against data tampering and ensures the integrity of the distributed ledger.

## Conclusion
The XOR-based Merkle aggregation combined with content-addressable anti-entropy mechanisms presents a robust solution for maintaining data integrity in distributed systems. Its efficiency, scalability, and strong security guarantees make it an attractive choice for modern decentralized applications.
